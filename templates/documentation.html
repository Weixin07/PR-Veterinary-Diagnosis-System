1. Introduction

Welcome to the Pet Recovery Veterinary Diagnostic System!

This system is designed to provide a seamless experience for managing veterinary cases, including diagnostic reports, case tracking, and user management. Our platform offers an intuitive interface that caters to veterinarians, assistants, and administrative staff, enabling efficient workflow and improved patient care.

System Overview

  • New Case Management: Assistants can enter new pet cases, recording essential health parameters for further diagnosis.
  • Case Editing and Review: Veterinarians have the ability to review and add additional information to ongoing cases.
  • Report Generation and Viewing: Users can view detailed reports containing diagnostic results and treatment suggestions.
  • User Account Management: Administrators can add, update, and delete user accounts, as well as manage their own account details.

2. System Requirements

  • Supported Browsers: Google Chrome (latest version), Mozilla Firefox (latest version), Safari (latest version), Microsoft Edge (latest version).
  • Internet Connection: A stable internet connection is required for system access and functionality.
  • Screen Resolution: A minimum screen resolution of 1280x768 is recommended for the best visual experience.
  • Credentials: Valid login credentials provided by your system administrator.

3. Getting Started

Accessing the Login Page

To start using the Pet Recovery Veterinary Diagnostic System, open your web browser and navigate to the system's login URL. Bookmark this page for future quick access.

Logging In

  1. Enter Email: Type in your assigned email address in the 'Email' field.
  2. Enter Password: Input your password in the 'Password' field. Click on the 'eye' icon to toggle password visibility if needed.
  3. Sign In: Click the 'Login' button to access the system.

If you encounter any login issues, please consult the 'Troubleshooting' section or contact your system administrator.

4. Feature Guides

4.1 Managing New Cases

For Assistants:

  1. Access New Case Form: From the home page, click on the 'New Case' link.
  2. Fill Out Case Details: Enter all the required information in the respective fields.
  3. Submit Case: After reviewing the details, click the 'Submit Case' button.

Fields Explained:

  • Activity: Describe the pet's current level of activity, from lethargic or non-responsive to hyperactive. Note changes from normal behaviour.
  • Breathing: Note any breathing irregularities, such as difficulty breathing, rapid breathing, or associated noises.
  • Eye Condition: Look for redness, discharge, cloudiness, or other eye abnormalities. Detail the discharge's colour and consistency.
  • Discharge: Report any discharge from the nose, eyes, ears, or other areas. Describe its colour, texture, and odour.
  • Diet & Weight: Record changes in appetite or weight, including eating habits and any weight fluctuations.
  • Skin & Coat Condition: Check for itching, flaking, redness, lumps, or unusual hair loss.
  • Ear Condition: Describe the ear's interior appearance, noting redness, swelling, discharge, or odour, and any discomfort shown by the pet.
  • Appetite: Detail any changes in the pet's appetite, such as increased or decreased eating, and specify if the pet has been refusing food altogether.
  • Vomiting/Diarrhoea: Mention the frequency and appearance of vomiting or diarrhoea. Note the colour, presence of blood, and any other notable characteristics.
  • Urination/Defecation Habits: Describe changes in urination or defecation patterns, including frequency, colour, and consistency. Indicate any signs of discomfort.
  • Behavioural Changes: Observe and report any changes in behaviour, such as increased aggression, anxiety, or lethargy. Note any unusual behaviours not typical for the pet.
  • Mobility: Note any difficulty in movement, such as limping, reluctance to move, or inability to stand or walk normally. Mention which limb(s) are affected if applicable.
  • Gum Colour: Examine the gums for any changes in colour (pale, blue, yellow, etc.). Pale or blue gums can indicate serious conditions and should be reported immediately.
  • Pain Response: Carefully observe the pet's reaction to gentle pressure on various body parts to assess pain. Note any vocalisation, withdrawal, or aggression as a response to touch.

4.2 Editing Existing Cases

For Veterinarians:

  1. Choose a Case: From the 'Case Review' section, click on the case you wish to edit.
  2. Add Information: Enter any additional observations or test results.
  3. Update Case: Click the 'Update Case' button to save your changes.

4.3 Viewing Reports

For All Users:

  1. Navigate to Reports: Select 'View Reports' from the home page.
  2. Select a Report: Click on the 'Report ID' that corresponds to the case you wish to review.
  3. Review Details: The detailed report will display, providing insights into the medical conditions, justifications, treatment suggestions, and references.

4.4 Managing User Accounts

For Administrators:

  1. Access Management: From the admin home page, select 'Manage Users'.
  2. Edit User Details: Click 'Update' next to a user to change their email or role.
  3. Save Changes: After editing, click the 'Update' button within the form to apply changes.

4.5 Adding New Users

For Administrators:

  1. Open the Add User Form: Click on 'Add User' from the admin home page.
  2. Enter User Information: Fill in the email, password, and assign a role.
  3. Create Account: Press the 'Add User' button to create the new user account.

4.6 Updating User Information

For Administrators:

  1. Find the User: In the 'Manage Users' section, locate the user whose information needs to be updated.
  2. Make Edits: Click on 'Update' to modify email or role details.
  3. Confirm Changes: Submit the form to save the new user details.

4.7 Deleting Users

For Administrators:

  1. Locate the User: In the 'Update & Delete Users' list, find the user to be deleted.
  2. Delete User: Click the 'Delete' button associated with the user's row.
  3. Confirm Deletion: A prompt will appear; confirm to proceed with user removal.

6. FAQ

Q: Can I access the system from my mobile device?

A: The system is best viewed on a desktop. While it may be accessible via a mobile browser, some features may not be fully supported.

Q: How secure is my data within the system?

A: We understand the importance of data security, especially when it comes to sensitive medical information. Our system employs several layers of security to ensure that your data is protected at all times:

  • Encryption: All sensitive data within the system is encrypted using advanced encryption standards. We use Fernet encryption, which is a symmetric encryption method that ensures that data such as passwords, medical reports, and personal information cannot be read by unauthorized parties.
  • Secure Transmission: Data sent between your device and our servers is encrypted using SSL/TLS protocols. This means that even during transmission, your data is secure and protected against eavesdropping and man-in-the-middle attacks.
  • Access Control: Users are only granted access to the parts of the system that are relevant to their role. For example, assistants have different access rights compared to veterinarians or administrative staff.
  • Audit Trails: All activity within the system is logged, creating an audit trail that can be reviewed in case of security incidents. This helps in ensuring accountability and tracing any changes made to the data.

7. Glossary

Query: In the context of our system, a query refers to the detailed information entered about a pet's condition, symptoms, and observed behaviours, which is used to facilitate diagnosis and treatment planning.

Fernet: A system of symmetric encryption used to ensure the security and privacy of data within the system. It encrypts data in such a way that it can only be decrypted by an entity with the correct key, thus protecting sensitive information from unauthorized access.

SSL/TLS Protocols: Secure Socket Layer (SSL) and Transport Layer Security (TLS) are cryptographic protocols designed to provide communications security over a computer network. In our system, these protocols encrypt data in transit between the user's device and our servers, safeguarding it from interception.

Symmetric Encryption: A type of encryption where the same key is used for both encryption and decryption of data. This method is used by Fernet, ensuring that data can be securely stored and accessed only by authorized parties.

Audit Trail: A record showing who has accessed the system and what actions they have taken during their session. This is crucial for security, compliance, and operational integrity, providing a way to trace any changes or accesses to sensitive data.

Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): A method of restricting system access to authorized users based on their role within the organization. Different roles have different levels of access to information and functionalities within the system.

Encryption Key: A piece of information that determines the output of a cryptographic algorithm. In encryption, a key specifies the particular transformation of plaintext into ciphertext, or vice versa during decryption.

Ciphertext: Data that has been transformed from plaintext into an unreadable format through encryption, ensuring that only authorized parties can decipher and understand it.
